The Approach

Good rendering cannot fix a distorted sketch. If the underlying proportions and perspective are inaccurate, the drawing will always feel wrong.

Cars are complex three-dimensional objects, and accurately placing their key lines by eye is extremely difficult. Without understanding the vehicle's structure and measuring its key landmarks, even a detailed rendering can appear distorted.

The first step is to establish accurate proportions. Learn to measure important landmarks such as the wheelbase, roofline, pillars, and wheel arches relative to eachother.

Once the structure is correct, perspective helps you understand how the car occupies space and how its forms change when viewed from different angles.

Start Improving

You need a strong foundation for realistic drawings. Master accurate proportions, learn to measure the key elements of a vehicle, and understand how to construct cars in a three-dimensional space.
